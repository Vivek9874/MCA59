There's someone to hear you out!


It's often hard for people to actively seek out help, which can be the result of various traumatic or disheartening past experiences. For such people, mental health and suicide-prevention can be a great resource to seek out help while keeping yourself anonymous.
A person's choice to remain anonymous shouldn't stop them to seek out for support, and that's why we've made a curated list of the resources we feel might help you out. If you feel a surge of negativity & depressive feelings, don't hesitate to contact any of the resources mentioned below, as they're trained individuals out there to support you in your difficult times. It's always better to talk about the difficulties you're going through, so as someone from these resources, or anyone you trust might be able to help you out of your agony.



Below are few recommended mental health and suicide prevention centers you can reach out for help.


1. Roshni Helpline
Contact: +91 4066202000
Roshni helpline is a Hyderabad-based suicide intervention centre.This NGO is a member of Befrienders Worldwide, an international organisation which is recognized by the United Nations. Offering free and confidential services, this organisation seeks to provide support to those who are battling emotional, behavioural, and psychiatric problems in life The volunteers are well trained to support someone suffering from any kind of mental illness.
They are available from 11 AM to 9 PM, Monday to Saturday.


2.Sneha Foundation India
Contact: +91 4424640050
Sneha Foundation India is a 24X7 operational helpline offering unconditional support to people who aren't mentally well.
This organisation works to lend emotional support to those in need. Be it anxiety, hypertension, or just any other mental illness that has been taking a toll on your peace of mind lately, you can approach its 24x7 operational suicide prevention helpline centre, which is based in Chennai.
You can also write to them at help@snehaindia.org.


3. Sumaitri Helpline
Contact: 011-23389090
Sumaitri will be your help when the world is shut down for you and you can't find anyone offering help nearby.
You can call them from 2 PM to 10 PM, Monday to Friday and 10 AM to 10 PM, Saturday-Sunday.
You can also write to them at: feelingsuicidal@sumaitri.net.


4. Aasra
Contact: 022 2754 6669
Aasra is a suicide-prevention and counselling NGO with a mission to help anyone who needs mental and moral support. The organisation selects volunteers who possess the right skill set to handle any situation that comes their way, train them intensively, and make them readily available to help you with your problems.
You can also write to them at: aasrahelpline@yahoo.com.


5. Arpita Foundation
Contact: 011-23655557
Arpita Foundation , started by a group of experienced volunteers in 2019, is a prominent organisation that aims to eliminate the stigma, spread awareness, and change the way one looks at mental health in India. With volunteers having several years of experience, the foundation doesn’t charge even a single penny for its services.
Whether you are looking for a one-on-one consultation or a telephonic one, find a permanent solution to what’s tampering with your mental health with this suicide prevention organisation. Their volunteers are proficient in Hindi, English, Gujarati, Punjabi, Bengali, Urdu, Konkani, Assamese, Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, and many other languages.
You can also write to them at: arpita.helpline@gmail.com


6. Sanjivini Society for Mental Health
Contact: +91 1124311918
Sanjivini Society for Mental Health in Delhi has been providing constant mental health support for the past 40 years. Being a fully volunteer-run organisation, it provides competent and free-of- cost services to people who are suffering from mental distress, irrespective of age, gender, or just any other social grounds. Their counselling services are done by skilled, trained, and knowledgeable volunteers who keep the consultation confidential, and go the extra mile to maintain a follow-up on your appointments.
You can also write to them at: sanjivini1971@gmail.com.


7. iCALL
Contact: 022-25521111
iCALL(Initiating Concern for All)is the brainchild of Tata Institute of Social Sciences (TISS). It provides professional, prudent, and unconditional emotional support to those who are suffering from psychosocial distress, such as anxiety, mood swings, eating disorders, identity crisis, addiction, and other such problems.
You can also write to them at: icall@tiss.edu.


8. Snehi
Contact: 011-26521415
Snehi Snehi is a non-governmental and not-for-profit organisation that provides mental health counselling. Started back in October of 1994, this iconic organisation’s primary focus is to help those who are victims of abuse, violence, and trauma. So far, it has managed to provide emotional and mental support to over 92,507 people across India, with the help of its trained student volunteers, working professionals, and donors.
You can avail either its telephonic counselling, which is free-of-cost, or face-to-face consultation by paying Rs.100 as a registration fee.
You can also write to them at: info@snehi.org, snehi.india@gmail.com.


9. Samaritans Mumbai
Contact: +91 84229 84528, +91 84229 84529, +91 84229 84530
Samaritans Mumbai happens to be the first Indian branch of Samaritans UK and actively provides mental health counselling to those in need. It offers three different types of services - Suicide Prevention Helpline, Face to Face consultation, and Professional Appointment consultation, all of which focus on helping you tackle any sort of mental illness.In fact, they keep the conversation confidential and non-judgemental, so you can discuss absolutely anything that is affecting your mental stability.
You can also write to them at: amaritans.helpline@gmail.com.


10. Vandrevala Foundation
Contact: +91 9999666555
Vandrevala Foundation is the first 24x7 helpline in the country that was manned by psychologists and had a documentation system in place since 2009. The organisation has helped transform the lives of more than 5,00,000 people before the pandemic struck! When the cases of COVID-19 started rising in the country last year, they upgraded their systems using unprecedented technology, which has enabled them to be multilocational (virtually) and more prompt than ever.
You can also write to them at: help@vandrevalafoundation.com.
